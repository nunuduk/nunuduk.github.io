Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of low-power IC design and advanced verification methodologies.

01

Ultra-Low-Power IC Design

Designing adiabatic and energy-recovering circuits — including standard cell libraries in SkyWater 90nm — that push computation to near-thermodynamic limits.

02

Advanced Verification

Developing UVM-based verification flows with bus functional models, constrained-randomization, and LLM-assisted coverpoint generation for thorough functional coverage.

03

Domain-Specific Architectures

Architecting accelerators like adiabatic systolic arrays for GeMM that trade conventional power budgets for specialized, orders-of-magnitude more efficient computation.

04

FPGA Prototyping & Post-Silicon Validation

Building FPGA test harnesses with custom UART datapaths and on-chip BRAM buffers for validating adiabatic processor designs before and after silicon tapeout.
