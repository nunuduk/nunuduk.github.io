About.
photo

I'm an electrical engineering student at the University of Notre Dame with a concentration in nanotechnology. I am a computer architecture researcher here at Notre Dame and I'm currently researching the implementation of an adiabatic microprocessor. My main contributions have been in adiabatic verification methodology. Beyond adiabatic circuits, I have great interests in digital accelerators.

I'm fortunate enough to say I'll be attending the University of Michigan ECE Ph.D program this fall. At UMich, I plan to continue research in large scale adiabatic CMOS circuits. Currently, I'm thinking about the adiabatic accelerators. We'll see where this goes in 5-6 years. Wish me luck!

Outside the lab, I'm involved in visual-communication design. I run design for Strike Magazine, help out with the Chinese Cultural Society and Dillon Hall merch, and created a fast-fashion clothing waste awareness campaign.

My Approach to Chip Design.

"As models scale beyond trillions of parameters, their demands on large-scale datacenters increase, intensifying their environmental impact with upkeep infrastructure serving only as "band-aid" solutions without sustainability in mind. I plan to address this challenge by following a core tenet of design thinking: address the problem at the source. Good design doesn't tell us to make cooler coolers, it tells us to make cooler chips."
- from my Statement of Purpose
