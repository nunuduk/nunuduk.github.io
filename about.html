About

I work in Notre Dame's chip design group, where I've spent the past two years designing and verifying adiabatic microprocessors. Day-to-day that means writing UVM testbenches, building constrained-random verification environments, and occasionally synthesizing standard cells in SkyWater 90nm.

Before that I was an NDNano Research Fellow, where I built an FPGA test harness for post-silicon validation — wiring everything together end to end, from on-chip BRAM buffers down to PC-side UART parsing.

Outside the lab I run design for Strike Magazine, help out with the Chinese Cultural Society, and mentor undergrads through their first IC design projects. On the side I build ML models — most recently a GRU text generator trained on 10,000+ pick-up lines.
